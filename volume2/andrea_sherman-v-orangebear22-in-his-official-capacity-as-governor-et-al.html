# RULING ON THE REQUEST FOR A TEMPORARY RESTRAINING ORDER AGAINST THE DECLARATION OF MARTIAL LAW BY THE GOVERNOR

It has become ever more apparent that to our Governor the rule of law is but an impediment to his policy goals. The rule of law is, according to him, something to navigate around or simply ignore. Whether that be for political or personal gain. Nevertheless, in this Court and in my chambers the rule of law is a bright beacon which I intend to follow. There are moments in the countrys history where people look back and ask:

Where were the lawyers? Where were the judges? In these moments, the rule of law becomes especially vulnerable. I refuse to let that beacon go dark today. As a judge, my job is not only to uphold the law but to protect the rule of law itself. Liberty and the writ of habeas corpus is a fundamental constitutional right. No amount of policy debate can change that. And the fact that the government has cloaked what is effectively a political decision as a legitimate constitutional is equally unconstitutional. The Constitution is not something with which the government may play policy games. Thats how our Constitution works and thats how the rule of law works. Because the Governors order attempts to circumscribe this process, it is clearly unconstitutional.

The temporary restraining order against the declaration of martial law is granted on a nationwide basis.

/x/ Alex J. Cabot
Associate Justice of the Supreme Court
Riding Justice of the District Court
