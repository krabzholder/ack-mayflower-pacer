On the parties cross-motions for summary judgment, the Court rules for Clark County.

The Plaintiff's claims are justiciable.

S.B. 350 2(a)(e) is unconstitutional, facially and as applied here: it delegates power to the Attorney General in violation of Article 6s command to prescribe by law, nullifies the constitutional independence of elected District Attorneys, and, as applied midterm, violates Article 11s guarantee of free and fair elections by effectively pulling a "bait-and-switch" on the electorate. For an election to be "free and fair" its effect must not be nullified by the state through stripping an elected officer of his core characteristics, thereby defeating the electorate's choice.

Plaintiffs crossmotion is GRANTED; the States motion is DENIED.

The Attorney Generals revocation order is VACATED, effective immediately, and the Attorney General is ENJOINED from revoking or reallocating the Clark County District Attorneys core prosecutorial authority absent legislatively prescribed standards consistent with the Constitution and the nondelegation doctrine. The District Attorney may enjoy the prosecutorial power set by law prior to any revocation.

This ruling does not affect 2(f) regarding civil representation and does not preclude the Senate from enacting a future prospective, constitutional framework.
