CIVIL COMPLAINT
1. Plaintiff received a traffic citation issued on 9-8-2025 for allegedly traveling 3 miles per hour above the posted speed limit on the hill going downwards towards mersea past the state police barracks.
2. At the time of the citation, Plaintiff had already disengaged the accelerator and was in the process of slowing down.
3. The cited violation occurred on a downhill grade, where natural momentum contributed to the vehicles speed.
4. Plaintiff was clocked by radar operated by the issuing officer, who had stationed himself at the bottom of said hill, a location which creates conditions ripe for unintentional minor speed deviations.
5. The penalty imposed for this alleged infraction was a fine of $300.
6. Plaintiff contends that a fine of $300 for a speed deviation of only 3 mph is excessive, disproportionate, and punitive, in violation of principles of fairness and proportionality.
7. Plaintiff further contends that the enforcement at this location constitutes arbitrary and unfair policing, targeting motorists for minor, unintentional deviations rather than addressing genuine safety risks.



PRAYER FOR RELIEF

Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court:

a. Dismiss the citation in its entirety, or in the alternative,
b. Reduce the fine to a nominal amount consistent with the minimal nature of the alleged violation, and
c. Grant such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.
d. Asks for 300 dollars from the defendant and/or 600 dollars from both the defendant and the police department.


Respectfully submitted,
