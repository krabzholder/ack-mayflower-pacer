PAPERLESS ORDER REGARDING MOTION TO SET ASIDE DEFAULT
The Court denies the Defendant's motion to set aside default judgment. Relief from judgment is governed under Rule 39 of the Civil Rules of Procedure. First, Defendant's motion lacks merit under 39(b)(1-3) which allows relief for a multitude of reasons, namely "mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or excusable neglect" under section 1. However, Defendant has not pled any of these in their motion, instead opting for a frivolous reading of Rule 7 of the Civil Procedure which governs service of process. To summon the Defendant, the summon command was utilized which provides a link to the server and thereby the complaint. It does not quite matter if the complaint was delivered in actuality. If the service allows the Defendant to access the complaint, that is enough. It is not the Plaintiff's nor the Court's job to lead the Defendant to the complaint as one may lead a horse to water, it is also not our responsibility to force defendants to drink the proverbial water. Furthermore, Defendant's motion is untimely under Rule 37. The same requires the Defendant to seek relief "within a reasonable time...and (3) not more than 5 days after the judgment, decree, order, or proceeding was entered or taken" 37(b). Given that default was entered on the 30th of June, the motion for relief came 12 days later, a full week after the timeframe set by Rule 37.
