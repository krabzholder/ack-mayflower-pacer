shah 2024-09-20 03:57 p.m.
I am finding probable cause in your affidavit.

My full order is attached onto the Trello entry. It reads the following;

Upon reviewing the affidavit of probable cause and the criminal information provided in the matter of State of Mayflower v. Tavvun, I have determined that there is sufficient evidence to establish probable cause for the charges filed against the defendant, Tavvun. This memorandum outlines the basis.

EVIDENCE REVIEWED
These charges arise from an incident on or about September 8, 2024, at Freedom Firearms in Mersea, where the defendant allegedly engaged in unlawful activities involving non-civilian-grade firearms and the unlawful detention of a victim, DeverusLazvari.

The prosecution has submitted the following evidence in support of the charges:
- Affidavit of Probable Cause filed by Supervisory Special Agent g_dsteel, which includes video evidence leading up o the alleged crimes, including the defendant overriding a search denial and placing non civilian firearms into the victims inventory
- Documentation establishing the defendant used physical force to place the victim in handcuffs and transport him in a vehicle without his consent.

FINDINGS
Upon reviewing the evidence, I find there is sufficient probable cause to believe that the defendant, Tavvun, committed the offenses as charged, specifically for:

- 1 M.S.C. 6201 – Unlawful dealing of a firearm
- 1 M.S.C 1301 – Gross misconduct
- 1 M.S.C 2202 – False Imprisonment
- 1 M.S.C. 2201 – Kidnapping

Based on the evidence presented, I find that probable cause exists for the charges brought against the defendant, Tavvun. Therefore, the case should proceed to trial.
