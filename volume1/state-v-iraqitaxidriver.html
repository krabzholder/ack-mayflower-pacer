Deverus 2024-11-21 06:20 p.m.
This matter having come before the Court on 21st of November, upon the prosecution’s request to toll the statute of limitations during the period of the defendant’s community removal, and the Court having reviewed the submissions and being otherwise fully advised in the premises, finds as follows: The Defendant, IraqiTaxiDriver, has fled the community, rendering him unable to be contacted or reached by any means where he could rejoin to stand trial and represent his interests in this case. In this circumstance, the Court finds that the total absence of the defendant entirely from criminal proceedings in a case where he is charged with felony offenses would be prejudicial to the interests of the defendant and that of justice. The defendant cannot enter a plea, appoint counsel, or make his own interests known to the court. The Court finds that the Defendant’s unavailability falls within circumstances justifying the tolling of the statute of limitations. The Court can further certify that the Prosecution has exercised due diligence in attempting to pursue this matter, but given the current status of the Defendant, it is appropriate to pause the running of the statute of limitations until such time as the Defendant becomes available. It is hereby ordered by this Court that: - The statute of limitations in this case is tolled from the date of the Defendant’s community removal, effective 21st of November 2024, and shall remain tolled for the duration of the Defendant’s unavailability due to community removal. - The statute of limitations shall resume upon the Defendant’s appeal to successful re-entry or availability within the jurisdiction. - The Prosecution shall promptly notify the Court of the Defendant’s re-entry. SO ORDERED, on the 21st Day of November, 2024. Signed, Hon. Judge acerxtro District Court Judge
