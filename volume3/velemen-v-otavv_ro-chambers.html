PAPERLESS ORDER
The Plaintiff moves to strike the Defendantâ€™s allegation of Felony Reckless Driving on grounds that the Defendant lacked a factual basis to support it at the time of the incident. The allegation requires proof of three violations, one of which the Defendant claims was speeding, based on a video clip (Ex. A) showing the Plaintiff for only 0.50 seconds from the perspective of the Defendant.

This Court finds that such a limited visual duration, unsupported by verifiable data or corroborating evidence (such as radar, pacer, etc.), is legally insufficient to support an allegation of speeding. Consequently, the requisite three violations necessary to sustain a claim of Felony Reckless Driving cannot be established.

Accordingly, the motion to strike the allegation of Felony Reckless Driving is GRANTED.
