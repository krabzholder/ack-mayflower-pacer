## ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE ##
RE: POTENTIAL DISQUALIFICATION OF COUNSEL
Cc: @huddy.

The Court, having reviewed the record in this matter, including the troubling deficiencies in the conduct of Defendants counsel, James M. Hamilton, finds cause to question whether counsels continued representation in this case is consistent with the Courts duty to ensure the fair administration of justice and to protect the integrity of its proceedings.

The Court is vested with inherent authority to regulate the conduct of attorneys appearing before it and to take measures necessary to preserve the orderly administration of justice. See Chambers v. NASCO, Inc., 501 U.S. 32, 43-46 (1991);* Kleiner v. First National Bank, 751 F.2d 1193, 1209 (11th Cir. 1985); Roadway Express, Inc. v. Piper, 447 U.S. 752, 764 (1980). This includes the power to disqualify counsel whose conduct threaten to prevent the court from carrying out its duties or deprive a party of effective representation.

The Court has identified a pattern of professional neglect by Mr. Hamilton that raises grave concerns, including but not limited to:

In this case (Yrn_Clxudz v. Pranavkedia97): Counsels scarce communications with both the client and the Court; submission of a defective settlement agreement contravening the Mayflower State Bar Code of Ethics r. 3.10 and Mayf. R. Civ. P. 33(b)(2)(f); persistent inattention and disregard for case management responsibilities.
In prior cases:

Deletevictim v. Offer_Sprite, No. CV-251-25: Dismissed with prejudice due to Mr. Hamiltons bad faith and failure to prosecute.
Eddeb v. BrendaPopplewell, No. CV-253-25: Record reflects ongoing failure to communicate with the Court and clients, resulting in material harm.

These incidents, taken together, suggest a recurring pattern of neglect, abandonment, and professional dereliction that directly implicates the Courts responsibility to safeguard the fairness and integrity of its proceedings.
singhski 2025-06-21 04:42 p.m.
Accordingly, it is hereby ORDERED that:

James M. Hamilton shall appear and SHOW CAUSE within 24 hours from the issuance of this Order as to why he should not be disqualified from continued representation in this matter and why this Court should not impose further sanctions, including but not limited to:

1. Referring this matter to the appropriate bar disciplinary authorities.
2.Entering additional remedial orders to protect the interests of the client and the Court.
3. Failure to timely respond to this Order shall be deemed a waiver of objection and may result in immediate disqualification and further sanctions without further hearing.

The Court emphasizes that this proceeding is not intended as punishment, but rather as a necessary measure to protect the clients right to effective advocacy and to uphold the dignity of this tribunal. See United States v. Dinitz, 538 F.2d 1214, 1219 (5th Cir. 1976); Musicus v. Westinghouse Electric Corp., 621 F.2d 742, 744 (5th Cir. 1980).

SO ORDERED.
/s/ Singhski
District Court Judge
