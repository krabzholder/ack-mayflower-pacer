@cabot

So to start with the basics: the destroyed trailer truck, also known as the Tobias Enforcer. This is a special vehicle which can only be purchased from the dealer at the docks. It's purchase price has been verified to be $50,000 UCD.

Since the defendant caused the destruction of plaintiff's truck, we ask to order the defendant to reimburse or otherwise compensate the plaintiff for the truck, worth $50,000.

Next, the truck was not in fact empty at the time of its destruction. There was cargo inside, a mix of aspen and cypress logs from what I recall. On their own they are unable to be sold, however they are turned into planks which can then be sold.

The estimated value of the cargo lost is around $124,500, which is
calculated by taking the total amount of storage units in use by the logs (1245 out of a possible 1500 total units of space), dividing it by the weight of a single log (15 lbs) and multiplying that by the logs would be selling prices. I used the following going-rate list for reference regarding the prices at the time, as I am not knowledgeable in the logging business: https://discord.com/channels/1183291978499235890/1371935265836498984/1372551545886867456

The amount was mixed, so it's not a simple mathematics formula we can use on this, the exact composition of the trailer can be seen in this exhibit (although it doesn't show the entire cargo haul inside): https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQPXloKINXA

We originally sued for $340,000 (this includes the cost of the trailer, cargo lost and compensation for the defendant's mental anguish he endured as a result of this blatant disregard for life and limb).

To conclude, we seek both compensatory damages to recover plaintiff's lost goods and punitive damages to punish and deter anyone with similar ideas in the future.
