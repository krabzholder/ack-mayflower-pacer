Richostein 2025-11-13 05:57 p.m.
Upon review of the States Motion to Substitute and Dismiss (Document 02), and the accompanying scope-of-office certification submitted pursuant to 5 M.S.C. 1 3209.43209.5, the Court finds that the statutory scheme relied upon by the Solicitor General has been expressly declared invalid, null, and void by binding authority. In ThatUniversal v. Clark County Sheriffs Office, CV-498-25 (Brenda Popplewell, J.) (Nov. 13, 2002), the District Court held that 3209.43209.7 impermissibly cloak state actors in sovereign immunity willy-nilly, contradict more than a century of constitutional doctrine, and are irreconcilable with Article X of the Mayflower Constitution. As Hopkins v. Clemson, 221 U.S. 636, 643 (1911), makes clear, sovereign immunity cannot be availed of by public agents when sued for their own torts, and courts cannot permit the State to create a privileged class free from liability for wrongs inflicted. The State may not, by certification alone, transmute an action between individuals into one against the sovereign, nor interpose immunity where the Constitution forbids it. Accordingly, the States Motion to Substitute the State of Mayflower as defendant and to dismiss the individual-capacity claims is DENIED in full. The 3209.43209.7 certification has no legal effect, carries no presumption, and confers no immunity. The individual-capacity claims against Governor Orangebear22 shall proceed. So Ordered.
