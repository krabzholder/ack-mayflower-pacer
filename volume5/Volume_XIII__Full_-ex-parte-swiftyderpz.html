We come before you today with an emergency ex parte application for habeas corpus, see In re BrendaPopplewell, No. CV-429-25 (Mayf. Dist. Ct. Aug. 9 2025). My client was arrested for 2 M.S.C. ¬ß 2102 "Operating a Commercial Vehicle without a CDL" for operating a vehicle "with a gross weight-unit rating of five-hundred (500) or more", which met the term of commercial vehicle under 6 M.S.C. 4 ¬ß 2102(a).

The statute for ¬ß 2102 of the vehicle code reads as follows:
Any person who drives a commercial vehicle designated by law, without a valid commercial drivers license issued by the state, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.

Under title 6's definition of a commercial vehicle, it has to meet any of the following:
(a) a vehicle with a gross weight-unit rating of five-hundred (500) or more;
(b) is designed to transport more than 15 persons, including the driver; or
(c) a vehicle that is specified by the game‚Äôs developers to require a Commercial Driver‚Äôs License to purchase.

The first clause, which is what my client was arrested under (by implication), violates due process since there is no not "know of the duty" to require a CDL, since it literally doesn't require it during purchase (see 1st screenshot vs 2nd screenshot, where a vehicle requiring a CDL for purchase clearly says it), and "there was no proof of the probability of such knowledge." State v. Winkelman, 2 Ohio App. 3d 465 (1981) (citing Lambert v. California, 355 U.S. 225, 229-30 (1957)).

Under Lambert, SCOTUS acknowledged the rule of ignorantia juris non excusat (ignorance of the law is not an excuse), but accordingly said that "due process places some limits on its exercise." 355 U.S. at 228.

The LOV on the arrest report (https://i.imgur.com/9rsfOs5.png) shows the arrest being effectuated, and the medal clip by my client (https://medal.tv/games/roblox/clips/lrXbAxuTfoYaeL9oj?invite=cr-MSxNckYsNDQ4Mzc4MTQ&v=63) shows them arresting him for the Lodger Courier Industrial (shown on the 1st screenshot), which has a gross-weight unit rating of exactly 500.

There is no possibility that Mx. Section 2102(a) is buried 500 lines deep into the sixth title of the state code - and doesn't even have a hyperlink in its corresponding offense in title 2, whereas some crimes have hyperlinks for obscure definitions. See, e.g., 1 M.S.C. ¬ß 1702.
