ORDER: On September 30, 2002, Defendant Derogatoryyy was held in contempt under 5 MSC  2104.1 for failure to pay a Default Judgment. Today, the State Senate enacted the Second Judiciary Act of 2002, SB393 (5th. Cong.) (the "Act") which amends Section 2104.1 to prohibit this Court from continuing to hold Defendant in contempt without, at least, "the unanimous consent of the [State Senate]." Id. The Court notes that there are core constitutional powers that no other branch of governmentexcept judgescan execute in the interests of the People. Some exclusive core powers include hearing expungements, handing down verdicts in criminal and civil cases, and provid[ing] the writ of habeas corpus. Mayfl. Const. art. X, 2. But other judicial functions are nominal in practice; their contours are loosely predicated on scholarly presumption. So understood, our Nations practice recognizes that some powers derive not from the Constitutions text, but inhere[] in the very nature of the courts themselves. In re SinisterInfernal, 2 Mayf. ___ (2025) (slip op. at 4). Among these, is a judges inherent authority to enforce its orders and keep the peace by holding contemnors in contempt [of court], id. See also Michaelson v. United States ex rel. Chicago, St. P., M. & O.R. Co., 266 U.S. 42, 6566 (1924) (The courts ... when called into existence and vested with jurisdiction over any subject, at once become possessed of [the power to punish for contempts].). While the Legislature may, of course, regulate this authority, it may not "so confine it as to render it ineffective or reduce it to mere impotence." Id (emphasis added).

Accordingly, it is is ORDERED
1. that the Act, in its entirety, is struck, sua sponte, as unconstitutional under Article X of the Mayflower Constitution;
2. that this Court's September 30, 2002, Order holding Defendant in contempt of court under 5 MSC 1 2104.1 is upheld; and
3. that the Clerk of Court must serve on the State Registrar a courtesy copy of this Order.

It is so ORDERED, DECREED, AND ADJUDGED in Chambers at Lander, Clark County, State of Mayflower, this 2nd day of October, 2002. Signed by District Judge Brenda Popplewell (Entered 10/2/2025).(edited)
