MINUTE ORDER: Before the Court is Plaintiffs Motion to Strike Defendants Answer. For the reasons that follow, the Motion is GRANTED IN PART and DENIED IN PART. It is elementary that contributory negligence applies only to actions for negligence; in actions for intentional injuries, it does not. Restatement (Second) of Torts 481 cmt. b (1965) ([A] plaintiff is not barred from a recovery against an intentional wrongdoer by his contributory negligence.). It is puzzling, then, why Defendant seeks to enshroud himself behind its shield. But it is nonetheless premature to preclude the Defendant from introducing evidence of traffic errors for the purpose of establishing comparative fault; in due time, the Court will consider that Motion separately, if it is forthcoming and warranted. Plaintiffs Motion is thus GRANTED, but only to the extent that it requests that Defendants Answer, insofar as Affirmative Defense II, be struck; in all other respects, it is DENIED. Accordingly, it is ORDERED that Defendants Answer, insofar as Affirmative Defense II, be STRUCK from the record. Signed by District Judge AwesomePIays (Entered: 12/21/2025).
